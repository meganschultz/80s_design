In the July 1980 issue of Kilobaud Microcomputing, publisher Wayne Green stated that "the best consumer ads I've seen have been those by Apple. They are attention-getting, and they must be prompting sale."

Launched on August 1, 1981, the original purpose of the channel was to play music videos guided by television personalities known as "video jockeys," or VJs.

The early 1980s saw Nintendo's video game division (led by Yokoi) creating some of its most famous arcade titles.

Cable News Network (CNN), an American basic cable and satellite television channel, was founded in 1980 becoming the first channel to provide 24-hour television news coverage, and the first all-news television network in the United States.

In the 1980s, Benetton was a youthful brand beloved for its sweaters and socially aware advertising.

TaB was a diet cola soft drink produced by The Coca-Cola Company after the successful sales and marketing of Diet Rite cola, previously the only sugarless soda on the market, owned by The Royal Crown Company; Tab was marketed to consumers who wanted to "keep tabs" on their weight.

During the 1980s, Reebok began expanding from tennis and aerobics shoes to running and basketball. The company also introduced sports clothing and accessories, along with a new line of children's athletic shoes bringing them some of the greatest success in the industry at the time.

Miami Vice was an American television crime drama series following two Metro-Dade Police Department detectives working undercover in Miami. The series ran for five seasons on NBC from 1984 to 1989. Unlike standard police procedurals, the show drew heavily upon 1980s New Wave culture and music. The show became noted for its integration of music and visual effects, and claimed by People Magazine to be the "first show to look really new and different since color TV was invented"

Esprit de Corp. designs, manufactures, and distributes a wide variety of apparel and fashion accessories for women and children soaring to high-profile success especially in the late 1980s. Renowned for fostering a sense of social responsibility, the company built its image on commitment to causes such as AIDS awareness and environmental conservation.

As the 1980s rolled around, Vans started to create shoes for a number of sports from baseball, basketball and wrestling to skydiving in an effort to compete with large athletic shoe companies. Vans Slip-Ons gained international attention and appeal when they were worn by Sean Penn in the 1982, iconic youth film “Fast Times at Ridgemont High” and increased their worldwide presence even further by 1988.

There was one must-have school supply on every kid's list each year during the 80s...a Trapper Keeper. Brightly colored three-ring binders that hold folders called Trappers and close with a flap. From the start, they were an enormous success. These organizational devices would come to define childhoods across North America as a matter of expression. With hundreds of amazing designs, there was a Trapper Keeper for every kid to express themselves.
