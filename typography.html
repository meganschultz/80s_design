Mistral: One of the earliest metal typefaces to successfully emulate informal handwriting designed by Roger Excoffon.

Flash: Designed by Edwin W. Shaar for Lanston Monotype

Reporter AKA Pinselschrift (brush script) Reporter: Designed by Carlow Winkow. The metal original — l arger sizes were also available in wood — came with many extras: alternates, swash underlines, ligatures, as well as logotypes for common German trigraphs like ‘sch’ and even whole syllables.

Company: Originally created by Martin Wait in 1978 for Letraset.

Motter Tektura: Created by Austrian designer Othmar Motter of Vorarlberger Graphik and distributed by Berthold Fototypes and Letraset.

Eagle: The Eagle series realizes the ideas behind designer, Morris Fuller Benton’s famous titling, Eagle Bold, the symbol of American recovery. Font Bureau Eagle was started in 1989 for Publish.

Glaser Stencil: Designed by Milton Glaser

Weiß-Antiqua AKA Weiss Roman: The original German name and spelling is Weiß-Antiqua, after its designer Emil Rudolf Weiß.

Candice: Designed by Alan Meeks for Letraset.

Palisade Graphic

Quicksilver: Dean Morris designed Quicksilver for Letraset - reminiscent of neon tube lettering, but actually meant to look like bent thermometers.

ITC Avant Garde Gothic: Herb Lubalin and Tom Carnase based their typeface on Lubalin’s logo for Avant Garde magazine. The Condensed was drawn by Ed Benguiat, and obliques designed by André Gürtler, Erich Gschwind and Christian Mengelt.
