The need for NIKE to build a system of graphics that would embody the spirit and motion of sport arrived in 1985 with the release of NIKE’s first all-over prints — a pink, white and red pattern derived from shoe outsoles, and gold barbells repeated on a black background. Extending beyond mere decoration, these initial designs united the brand’s values with external influences like cultural trends, historical references and global sporting events.
